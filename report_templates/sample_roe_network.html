Rules of Engagement (ROE)

Network Penetration Test

1. Introduction and Authorization

This document outlines the formal agreement and Rules of Engagement (ROE) for a network penetration test engagement between Global Corp ("the Client") and GScapy Security Team ("the Tester"). The Client explicitly authorizes the Tester to perform security testing against the assets defined within the scope of this document.

2. Period of Performance

All testing activities will be conducted between:

Start Date & Time: 2025-11-10 09:00 EST

End Date & Time: 2025-11-21 17:00 EST

Any testing performed outside of this window is unauthorized unless explicitly agreed upon in writing.

3. Scope of Testing

3.1. In-Scope Networks & Hosts

The following network ranges and hosts are authorized for testing:

  • Internal Corporate Network: 10.10.0.0/16
  • DMZ Network: 172.16.1.0/24
  • Specific External IP: 203.0.113.55 (Primary Firewall)

3.2. Out-of-Scope Networks & Hosts

The following assets are strictly out of scope. The Tester will make every effort to avoid impacting these systems:

  • Industrial Control Systems (ICS) Network: 10.20.0.0/16
  • VoIP Phone System

3.3. Specific Exclusions

The following activities are explicitly forbidden:

  • Physical access attempts to any facility.
  • Denial of Service (DoS) or Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks.
  • Destructive actions against any system.
  • Social engineering of Client employees beyond phishing simulation agreed upon separately.

4. Methodology & Allowed Tools

The Tester will follow a standard penetration testing methodology (NIST SP 800-115). Testing will involve reconnaissance, scanning, enumeration, exploitation, and post-exploitation phases.

A non-exhaustive list of tools that may be used includes:

Nmap, Masscan, Metasploit Framework, Responder, Impacket, CrackMapExec, BloodHound.

5. Communication & Reporting

Primary Client Contact: Robert Paulson, r.paulson@globalcorp.com, 555-0123

Primary Tester Contact: Lead Tester, security@gscapy-team.local, 555-0321

The Tester will immediately report any critical vulnerabilities or suspected system compromises to the Primary Client Contact. A final, detailed report will be delivered within 10 business days of the engagement's conclusion.

6. Data Handling

All data collected during the engagement will be encrypted and stored securely. All client data will be permanently destroyed within 30 days after the final report has been delivered and accepted, unless otherwise requested in writing.
